Introduction

In 1890, Jose Rizal annotated and republished Antonio de Morga's historical account "Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas" (Events in the Philippine Islands), originally published in 1609.

This scholarly work became one of Rizal's most important contributions to Philippine historiography, challenging colonial narratives and reclaiming pre-colonial Filipino identity.

2 / 8

Who Was Antonio de Morga?

  • Spanish lawyer and colonial official who served in the Philippines from 1595-1603
  • Held positions as Lieutenant Governor-General and judge of the Royal Audiencia
  • Wrote "Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas" in 1609, providing an eyewitness account of Philippine affairs
  • His work covered Philippine history, culture, Spanish colonization, and interactions with neighboring Asian countries
3 / 8

Why Rizal Chose Morga's Work

1. Objective Historical Account

Unlike friar-written histories, Morga was a secular official whose account was more factual and less biased by religious motives.

2. Evidence of Pre-Colonial Civilization

Morga's work contained descriptions of advanced Filipino culture, trade, and governance before Spanish colonization.

4 / 8

Why Rizal Chose Morga's Work (continued)

3. Counter-Narrative Tool

Rizal used Morga's own words to challenge Spanish claims that they "civilized" barbaric Filipinos.

4. Scholarly Credibility

As a Spanish official's account, it carried authority that would be difficult for colonial critics to dismiss.

5. Awakening National Consciousness

By showcasing Filipino achievements before colonization, Rizal aimed to inspire pride and national identity.

Excerpts from Morga's Work

"The natives of these islands were well-built and of good disposition... They had their own system of writing and letters."
"They carried on considerable trade with China, Japan, Siam, Malacca, and the Moluccas, exchanging gold, wax, pearls, and other products."

These passages demonstrated that Filipinos had established culture, literacy, and international commerce before Spanish arrival.

Examples of Rizal's Annotations

On Spanish "Civilization":

"The Spaniards found the Filipinos already civilized. The Filipino had his own laws, literature, and system of government. He was not the savage that the friars depicted."

On Filipino Capability:

"The Filipino of today is no less capable than his ancestors. What he needs is not foreign guardianship but the opportunity to develop his abilities."

Historical Significance

  • Intellectual Resistance: Rizal's annotations were a scholarly form of resistance against colonial propaganda
  • Source of Pride: Revealed a dignified Filipino past, countering inferiority complexes imposed by colonizers
  • Methodological Innovation: Pioneered Filipino historical scholarship using primary sources
  • Inspiration for Nationalism: Strengthened the foundation for Philippine independence movement
  • Enduring Legacy: Remains essential reading for understanding Philippine history and identity
